流利说课文原文 - Level3.Unit3

PART - 2

Sciences

  1. Biology(生物学) is the study(研究) of life,including its structure(结构、构造) and evolution(演化、进化).

  2. Biologists(生物学家) study how life survives(生存) and reproduces(繁衍、繁殖).

  3. Chemistry is the study of matter,including the structure of atoms and molecules.

  4. Chemists(化学家) study how various(各种各样的 = a variety of) substances interact(相互作用) with(和…相互作用) each other.

  5. Astronomy(天文学) is one of the oldest sciences.

  6. Astronomers study the structure and evolution of the universe,including the study of starts(恒星),planets(行星) and galaxies(星系).

  7. Geology(地质学) is the study of the structure and history of the Earth and other planets.

  8. Geologists study different types of rocks,earthquakes and the different layers of the Earth.

  9. Mathematics(数学) is the study of numbers,shapes(形状、图形),patterns(模式、规律) and change(变化).

  10. Mathematics is used by all other branches(分支) of science.

PART - 3

Sources of Energy

  1. Solar(太阳能的、日光的) energy is one of the cleanest and most plentiful(丰富的、充裕的) sources of energy.

  2. Solar power depends on sunlight, so in cloudy weather and at night, no power is generated.

  3. Windy energy is non-polluting but is only useful in places where there is a lot of wind.

  4. Wind turbines(涡轮机 /ˈtɜːrbaɪn/ ) convert the kinetic(动力的 /kɪˈnetɪk/ ) energy of the wind into mechanical(机械 /məˈkænɪkl/) power.

  5. Nuclear(核能 /ˈnuːkliər/) energy is efficient and does’t produce carbon gases and a waste product.

  6. The dangers of nuclear power include deadly(致命的) radioactive(放射性) waste products.

  7. A major source of energy comes from the burning of fossil(化石 /ˈfɑːsl/) fuels(燃料 /‘fjʊəl/ ),such as coal and oil,

  8. When we burn fossil fuels, waste gases, such as CO2 are produced.

  9. Fossil fuels remain the largest source of energy for most countries.

  10. Hydropower(水力发电 /‘haɪdro,paʊɚ/) comes from the kinetic energy of falling water.

  11. Output is reliable and can be regulated(调节、校准 /‘rɛgjə,let/) to meet the demand(要求、需求 /dɪˈmænd/),except during periods(周期、时期 /pɪrɪədz/) of drought(干旱 /draʊt/).

  12. Dams(水库、大坝 /dæm/) are expensive to build and affect(/əˈfekt/) wildlife such as fish.

Types of Words

  1. Here are some different types of words in english.

  2. Words that are nouns(名词 /‘naʊnz/) or pronouns(代词 /‘pronaʊn/) are used to represent objects.

  3. A noun or a pronoun can be a person,an animal or a thing.including an idea.

  4. Verbs are used to express(表达、快递) actions such as to sit down or stand up.

  5. We also use verbs to express relationships, such as to love someone or to own something.

  6. We use adjectives(形容词 /ˈædʒɪktɪv/) to describe objects,such as a tall building.

  7. Adjectives are used with nouns and pronouns, but not with verbs.

  8. We use adverbs to describe actions,such as to run fast or to walk slowly.

  9. Adverbs express the quality of an action,such as how well or poorly something is done.

  10. We use conjunctions(连词 /kən’dʒʌŋkʃən/) to connect thins or actions,such as to read and write.

  11. Conjunctions include words such as and, or, because, but and yet.

PART - 4

Leonardo da Vinci 1

  1. Leonardo da Vinci is one of the greatest geniuses of all time.

  2. He was a painter,an architect(建筑师 /ˈɑːrkɪtekt/),an engineer and a scientist.

  3. He was born(/bɔːrn/) in Vinci, Italy,in 1452.

  4. Vinci is a town(/taʊn/) just outside of the great Italian city,Florence.

  5. His name,da Vinci,means “of Vinci”.

  6. Leonardo’s father was a lawyer and landowner(地主 /ˈlændoʊnər/),and his mother was a peasant(农民 /ˈpeznt/).

  7. Leonardo’s parents were never married to each other.

  8. Leonard lived with his mother until he was around five years old.

  9. When he was five,he moved into the home of his father.

  10. By then his father had married a 16-year-old girl.

  11. Leonard’s mother married another man and moved to another town.

  12. She had many more children after that,with several different men.

  13. In the end,Leonardo had more than 15 half sisters and brothers.

  14. As a young man,Da Vinci didn’t go to school.

  15. He was educated(/ˈedʒukeɪtɪd/) at home in reading,writing and mathematics.

  16. In other subjects,he was mostly self-educated.

  17. He had access to books at his father’s home.

  18. Leonardo spent a lot of time outdoors and developed a strong interest in nature.

  19. He love to observe things,especially birds.

Leonardo da Vinci 2

  1. Leonardo’s early drawings and paintings demonstrated(证明、表明 /ˈdemənstreɪt/) a rare(/rer/) talent.

  2. His father and his father’s friends recognized(意识到 /‘rɛkəg’naɪzd/) this talent and encouraged him.

  3. When he was 15,he was sent to Florence.

  4. In Florence,he became an apprentice(学徒 /əˈprentɪs/) to a famous master painter.

  5. For the next few years,Leonardo worked at his master’s workshop(车间、工厂).

  6. It wasn’t long before his ability surpassed that of his master.

  7. Some people say that his master became jealous of Leonardo’s great talent.

  8. They say that the master vowed(发誓 /vaʊ/) to never paint again!

  9. In 1478,with his father’s help,Leonardo set up his own workshop.

  10. In 1482,he entered the service of a powerful man in the city of Milan(意大利米兰).

  11. This man was the Duke(公爵 /duːk/) of Milan.

  12. For the next few years Leonardo designed buildings,machinery(机械 /məˈʃiːnəri/) and weapons of war.

  13. Weapons were important because Italy was constantly(不断地、时常的 /ˈkɑːnstəntli/) at war during this period.

  14. From 1485 to 1490,Leonardo produced designs for a variety of weapons.

  15. In his notes,he predicted(预言 /prɪˈdɪktɪd/) the development of advanced(先进的 /ədˈvænst/) weapons such as submarines(潜水艇 /‘sʌbmə,rin/) and flying machines.

  16. In 1499,the French invaded Italy and Leonardo left Milan.

  17. Altogether,Leonardo spent 17 years in Milan.

  18. During this period,he spent much of his time studying in nature.

  19. He dissected(解剖 /daɪˈsekt/) bodies,both human and animal,to study and illustrate(阐释、说明 /ˈɪləstreɪt/) anatomy(解剖学 /əˈnætəmi/).

  20. His detailed(详尽的、详细的) illustrations are masterpieces(杰作).

  21. Leonardo filled many notebooks with drawings and ideas.

  22. He wrote backwards,so you need a mirror to read them.

  23. After 1500,Leonardo spent time in a number of Italian cities.

  24. He worked very slowly,and the range of his interests was very wide.

  25. As a result,leonardo left many paintings and projects unfinished.

  26. One painting that he did finish was the Mona Lisa.one of the world’s most famous paintings.

  27. People are still interested in this painting,500 years after he painted it.

  28. Though there are many theories(理论、学说 /‘θɪərɪz/),nobody knows who the woman in the painting really was.

  29. In 1516 Leonardo left Italy for good when the French King, Francis 1,offered him a position.

  30. There ,he had the freedom to paint and draw whatever he wanted.

  31. Leonardo died in 1519,at the age of 67.

  32. Some say that the French King,who had become a close friend,was with him when he died.

  33. He was buried in a church which was destroyed during the French Revolution.

  34. The exact location of his remains(人的遗体) is unknown.

Sources of Pollution

  1. Waste water and carbon gases from farm animals are bad for the environment.

  2. Animal wastes and other chemicals flow into the ground and pollute water supplies(供应) (水源、供水系统).

  3. Exhaust gases from automobiles(汽车 /‘ɔːtəməbilz]/) are a major cause of air pollution and global warming.

  4. As a result,many companies are trying to produce more efficient and cleaner cars.

  5. Pesticides(杀虫剂 /‘pɛstɪsaɪdz/) and fertilizers(肥料 /ˈfɜːrtəlaɪzər/) are used by farmers to grow crops(农作物、庄家) such as fruits and vegetables.

  6. These chemicals pollute water supplies when they flow into the ground or rivers(/ˈrɪvər/).

  7. Factories like this one allow harmful chemicals to get into the air or water.

  8. Companies need to try harder to protect the environment and our health.

  9. Nuclear waste products are radioactive and can pollute the environment for thousands of years.

  10. They need to be safely transported and stored in a safe place.

Historical Figures 历史人物

  1. The British(/ˈbrɪtɪʃ/) writer, Jane Austen, was born in England in 1775, one of seven children.

  2. She wrote some of the most popular love stories of all time.

  3. One of the most famous female rulers in history, Cleopatra(《埃及艳后》克利欧佩特拉 /klɪə’pætrə/) had affairs(风流韵事 /əˈfer/) with Julius Caesar(尤利乌斯•凯撒) and later with Mark Antony(马克·安东尼).

  4. She was known for her great beauty and charming voice.

  5. Born in 1756, Mozart composed(创作) and performed(演奏) some of the world’s greatest classical music.

  6. He was the youngest of seven children and by the age of five he was already composing music.

  7. The son of a King, Alexander the Great(亚历山大大帝) was a military(军事 /ˈmɪləteri/) leader who created one of the largest empires(帝国 /ˈempaɪər/) in the ancient(古代、古老 /ˈeɪnʃənt/) world.

  8. As a youth(青少年时期 /juːθ/),Alexander was taught by the Greek(希腊 /ɡriːk/) philosopher(哲学家 /fəˈlɑːsəfər/) Aristotle(亚里士多德 /‘æristɔtl/).

  9. Mahatma(圣雄、圣人 /məˈhɑːtmə/) Gandhi(甘地) was a 20th century(世纪 /ˈsentʃəri/) leader who used nonviolent(非暴力的 /,nɑn’vaɪələnt/) resistance(抵抗、反抗 /rɪˈzɪstəns/) to lead India to independence.

  10. Gandhi dedicated(专注、献身 /ˈdedɪkeɪtɪd/) his life to the pursuit(追求 /pərˈsuːt/) of truth.
