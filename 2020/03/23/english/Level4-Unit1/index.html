流利说课文原文 - Level4.Unit1

PART - 1

Visiting a Friend 1

  1. Christina is on a two-week vacation.

  2. This is the first vacation she has had for a long time.

  3. The last vacation she took was more than two years ago.

  4. Now she is visiting a friend in San Francisco.

  5. Her friend and her husband are living near the center of the city.

  6. He works for a start-up company in Silicon Valley.

Landforms 地貌

  1. Mountains are landforms higher than 2000 feet, or about 600 meters, above sea level.

  2. Hills are like mountains, but not as tall.

  3. Mountains are formed by forces deep within the Earth and are made of many types of rocks.

  4. Rivers are streams of water that usually begin in mountains and flow into the sea.

  5. Many early cities were built alongside(在旁边) rivers.

  6. Most of the world’s water is in the oceans,which are large bodies of salt water.

  7. The oceans are also an important source of oxygen.

  8. Deserts are areas of land that receive little or no rainfall(雨量).

  9. The amount of rainfall in some deserts is so low that only a few plants can survive.

  10. A valley(山谷 /ˈvæli/) is a low, relatively(相对的 /ˈrelətɪvli/) flat area(相对平坦的区域) between hills or mountains, often with a river running through it.

  11. Some valleys are formed by flowing water, and others are formed by glaciers(冰川 /‘gleʃɚ/).

  12. Many early civilizations(文明 /ˌsɪvələˈzeɪʃn/) developed near rivers.

PART - 2

A Trip to Yosemite Park 1

  1. Today Christina and her friend are enjoying themselves.

  2. This morning they got up very early and drove out of San Francisco.

  3. They crossed the Bay Bridge(海湾大桥) before the early morning commute(通勤 /kəˈmjuːt/) and drove south.

  4. Because it was so early,there were almost no cars.

  5. After driving for an hour and a half, they stopped and had breakfast.

  6. They ordered pancakes(薄烤饼) and coffee.

  7. They also had some fresh fruit.

  8. When they finished eating, it was 7 o’clock.

  9. Then they got back into the car and drove south.

  10. Christina wanted to listen to music, so they turned on the radio.

  11. An hour later they stopped for gas.

  12. They noticed that there was also a charging station for electric cars.

  13. it’s now 1:15 and they are in Yosemite Park.

  14. They have been there for two hours, since 11:15.

  15. They are looking at Yosemite Falls, which is the highest waterfall in park.

  16. The water drops a total of 740 meters from the top of the upper fall to the base of the lower fall.

A Trip to Yosemite Park 2

  1. In another 15 minutes, they plan to eat lunch at magnificent(壮丽的；华丽的；宏伟的 /mæɡˈnɪfɪsnt/) old hotel in the park.

  2. It was built in 1927 and has a great view.

  3. They wanted to stay there, but they couldn’t get reservations.

  4. To get a room at that hotel, you usually need to make reservations several months in advance(预先，提前).

  5. They plan to stay in the park until around 4 o’clock.

  6. By then they will have been in the park for nearly 5 hours.

  7. Then they’ll get back into the car and drive back to San Francisco.

  8. They plan to eat dinner somewhere along the way.

  9. If the traffic isn’t too bad,they should be back in San Francisco by 10 o’clock.

  10. Early tomorrow morning they are going to cross the Golden Gate Bridge and go north along the coast.

  11. From some places, you can look out on the ocean and see whales and other sea life.

  12. Christina has never seen a whale before, so she’s looking forward to it.

  13. And tomorrow night they have something special planned.

  14. They are going to watch a baseball game.

  15. Christina has never seen a baseball game, but she has heard that it is very popular in the US.

  16. And the San Francisco baseball team is one of the best.

Science

  1. A force is a push or a pull on an object.

  2. A force changes the motion(移动 /ˈmoʊʃn/) of a moving object.

  3. A force can be strong or weak, and it always has a direction.

  4. The Earth is a planet that goes around the sun in a circular orbit(n. 轨道 vt. 绕…轨道而行 /ˈɔːrbɪt/).

  5. The Earth has a layer of gas above its surface, which helps to support life.

  6. Atoms are the building blocks(积木，建筑模块) of matter.

  7. At the center of an atom is its nucleus(原子核 /ˈnuːkliəs/), which has a positive electric charge.

  8. Outside the nucleus are electrons, which have a negative charge.

  9. Asteroids(小行星 /ˈæstərɔɪd/) and comets are objects that orbit our Sun but are smaller than planets.

  10. Asteroids that pass close to the Earth are called near-earth objects.

  11. The layer of gases just above Earth’s surface is our atmosphere.

  12. The atmosphere is made up of gases such as oxygen and nitrogen.

  13. Without an atmosphere, there would be no life on Earth.

Geometry(几何学 /dʒiˈɑːmətri/)

  1. The two lines on the left intersect(相交 /ˌɪntərˈsekt/) each other at a right angle, which is 90 degrees.

  2. The two lines on the right are parallel(平行 /ˈpærəlel/), which means they never cross.

  3. The adjacent(相邻的 /əˈdʒeɪsnt/) sides of a rectangle are perpendicular(垂直 /ˌpɜːrpənˈdɪkjələr/), which means they meet at a 90-degree angle.

  4. The opposite(对立面 /ˈɑːpəzɪt/) sides of a rectangle are equal and parallel, which means they never cross.

  5. The area of a rectangle is the length times(乘以 prep.介词) the width.

  6. A circle is 2 dimensional(空间 /dɪmenʃənl/) figure(图形 /ˈfɪɡjər/) with a radius(半径 /ˈreɪdiəs/) and a circumference(圆周，周长 /sərˈkʌmfərəns/), which is the distance around the circle.

  7. The area of a circle is pi r squared, where r is the radius and pi is the ratio(比率，比例 /ˈreɪʃioʊ/) of a circle’s circumference to its diameter(直径 /daɪˈæmɪtər/).

  8. A sphere is a three dimensional figure with a radius and a volume, which is 4/3(four thirds) pi r cubed(立方 /kjuːb/).

  9. All points on the surface of a sphere are equidistant(距离相等 /ˌekwɪˈdɪstənt/) from its center.

  10. A right triangle is a 2 dimensional figure with 3 sides, two of which are perpendicular.

  11. To find its area, multiply the lengths of the two perpendicular sides, a and b, and divide by two. A = 1/2(one a half) ab.

PART - 3

Harry’s Trips 1

  1. Harry is an American businessman.

  2. His job requires him to do a lot of traveling.

  3. He takes 9 or 10 trips a year.

  4. In the past six months he has made 5 trips.

  5. Three were to Asia and two were to Europe.

  6. The purpose of these trips is to attend meetings and make presentations(讲座).

  7. Harry loves to travel and he has traveled to many countries.

  8. Most of his trips are for one or two weeks.

  9. His first international trip was 15 years ago when he was 20 years old.

  10. He really wanted to see Asia,so he and a friend flew to Tokyo, Japan.

  11. From Tokyo, they traveled around Japan for 2 weeks.

  12. The people they met were very nice, but most of them didn’t speak English.

  13. Harry tried to speak a little Japanese, but it was difficult.

  14. He didn’t do very well.

  15. When he spoke to people, most of them just looked at him.

  16. They didn’t understand what he was trying to say.

  17. From Japan they went to Korea for a week, and then they went to China.
